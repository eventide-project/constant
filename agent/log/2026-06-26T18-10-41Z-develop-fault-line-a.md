# Developed fault-line A (options the AI can't produce): two kinds of absent option — options AVAILABLE to the AI (it can produce them, just missed this pass; fixable by running several independent lists from different angles) and options UNAVAILABLE to the AI (only the person can produce them; never reached however much the AI improves). The unavailable kind can't be produced, only kept from suppressing the person: showing a list makes the person pick from it instead of producing their own, so the remedy is answer-first-compare-second (the AI's list shown after, as a cross-check) — the same instrument as the conditioning-confidence blind audit. A sliver of self-knowledge survives: the AI can tell when its list is all of one kind (stuck, missing categories) though not the missing content. Unification: the option the AI can't produce, the decision it can't recognize as needing the person, and the answer it can't predict are the same single thing seen three ways — what only the person can produce; A is handed to the person, not closed
