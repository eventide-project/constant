# Observation: "whether to interject" is the prior question — and it is a second dial, not the hinge marks

**Status:** Working hypothesis, under active discovery. Not a binding rule. Opens a question upstream of the hinge/gate framework: the framework answers *where* the human interjects (hinges) and *how* (deliberation at gates), but presupposes *that* the human interjects. Promote/fold into the human-in-the-loop rule once the gate-policy axis is settled. Companion to `2026-06-20T18-48-32Z-tdd-hinge-marks-and-discovery.md` (the marks) and `2026-06-20T18-55-29Z-settling-the-hinge-marks.md` (soft-spot B / affordability).

## The point

It remains possible to have the AI proceed **entirely unscrutinized** — write the efferent call, judge solubility, resolve every hinge itself, generate straight through with no human gate. So the live question in TDD-AI is not only *where* to interject but **whether** to interject at all. The "whether" is upstream of the "where."

## Why this is a distinct axis (two dials)

The hinge marks (**subtle** + **load-bearing**) locate *leverage*, not *necessity*. They identify where human judgment pays off most **given the human is in the loop**; they do not establish that the human must be in the loop. The AI can make a subtle, load-bearing call unscrutinized — it just makes it without the taste/intent the subtle mark says lives only in the human.

So the framework has two dials, rightly separated:

1. **Hinge-ness** — is this decision subtle ∧ load-bearing? A fact about the *design*. Capability-independent (this is what soft-spot A established). Tells you *where the risk is concentrated*.
2. **Gate-policy** — given a hinge, do we actually stop and hand it over? Capability-**dependent** and cost-dependent. This is affordability (soft-spot B) **plus** how much of the subtle knowledge has already been transferred into the AI's conditioning. Tells you *whether to pay to cover the risk*.

The whether-question is dial 2. The earlier work pushed capability *out* of dial 1 on purpose; capability re-enters here, legitimately.

## The tension that keeps "whether" from being free

"Subtle" is defined as *absent from the artifacts, present only in the human*. If that holds at a genuine hinge, an unscrutinized AI there can only **average** — which the thesis forbids ("don't regress to the mean; efferent in, efferent out"). So at a real hinge, proceeding unscrutinized = proceeding *without the thing that resolves it correctly*. That is an argument that, **at hinges specifically**, you should interject — which threatens to collapse "whether" back into "where."

## The escape hatch (already in the thesis)

"Condition on this project's high-solubility code" is an attempt to *pre-load* the subtle knowledge into the AI's conditioning, so the hinge is already-resolved before the loop reaches it. To that degree the gate is pure overhead. Hence: **the better the conditioning, the fewer the gates.** "Unscrutinized" is not the absence of the framework — it is the framework with gate-policy dialed to *trust*, valid exactly to the degree conditioning has absorbed the subtlety. Plausible terminal aim: drive gates toward zero by transferring subtle knowledge into conditioning, leaving interjection as the fallback for what has not transferred yet.

## The irreducible floor (recursion)

"Whether to interject" is itself the most load-bearing hinge there is. By the framework's own logic it must be deliberated — at least once, by the human, to set the policy. A fully unscrutinized run is reachable, but only *downstream* of one human act that licenses it: the human can delegate the loop, but cannot delegate the decision to delegate without first making it. That one interjection is the floor that cannot go to zero.

## Open / next
