# Present hinge choices through the AskUserQuestion selection UI; its built-in escape is the chat option

At every hinge gate, hand the choices to the human through the interactive selection UI (the `AskUserQuestion` tool) — not prose, not a hand-typed list. Each genuine candidate is an option.

**Do not add a "Chat about this" option of your own.** The selection UI auto-supplies a free-text "Other" escape; that built-in escape *is* the mandatory chat/dictate origination escape. Adding your own duplicates it.

The UI requires **at least two options**. Do not manufacture cosmetic options to reach the floor (per the actuation-gate rule). Instead, when the shape looks pinned, look harder for the **real underlying decision** the human should originate — e.g. an interface contract choice (a keyword required vs. defaulting), a return-type choice, an identity-vs-value choice. A genuine hinge almost always has a real second candidate once examined; surface it. The built-in "Other" escape then carries the chat path, so the human is never boxed into the option set.

**Why:** The selection UI makes the handoff legible and already provides the free-text origination escape; routing genuine candidates through it keeps the options-with-escape mechanism consistent without duplicating the escape. The two-option floor is a forcing function — it pushes toward finding the real decision rather than presenting a fait-accompli single proposal (Law 1: provoke origination, not mean-bias).

**How to apply:** Present every hinge's choices via `AskUserQuestion`, one option per genuine candidate, never adding your own "Chat about this" (the built-in "Other" is it). Find at least two genuine candidates by surfacing the real underlying decision; never pad with cosmetic variants. Applies at every hinge of the cycle — actuation, assertion, controls, implementation, naming. Related: the actuation-gate options-or-chat rule and the hinge-cycle rule.
